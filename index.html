Origin and Myth

One version credits advertising executive Frederick R. Barnard, who attributed the phrase to an ancient Chinese proverb.

The closest Chinese equivalent translates to “Hearing something a hundred times isn't better than seeing it once.”

Power of Visuals

The issue with saying “a picture is worth a thousand words” is it sets up a false battle between words and images. It suggests one image can match, replace, or outdo the impact of words

Words and images function differently; they communicate in distinct ways and have their unique capacities for conveying nuance, detail, and emotion.

The Relationship

Instead of pitching images and text against each other, we need to learn when to use which, and how to use both images and words to strengthen each other. Using your text and visuals in a way that complements each other can make or break the story you're trying to tell.

Images and words are different forms of language. One can express that which the other cannot.”

Dos and Don'ts

  1. Prioritize Information
  2. Choose visuals that tell a story
  3. Be careful when adding one picture after the other
  4. Rehearse to Get a Feeling for the Impact of the Images
  5. Ask Others How They Feel About Your Visuals
